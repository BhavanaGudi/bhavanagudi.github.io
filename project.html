AI-enabled entry gates

This project combines both Deep Learning and IoT technologies. The underlying model uses a a Deep CNN to identify masked and unmasked people. The IoT sensor - Infrared sensor (used to detect body temperature) and processor - Raspberry Pi used to collect the data and take the necessary actions.

The CNN model was trained on numerous masked and unmasked images collected during the project. The network was built and trained using Tensorflow. The model was trained over 2K images and achived an accuracy of about ~91%.

The IR temperature sensor was used to detect the body temperature of an individual. The normal body temperature is considered to be around 37°C and any temperature above that would restrict the access of any individual entering the premise. Unmasked individuals (detected from the CNN model) would also be prevented from entering the premises.

Demand Forecasting

The project aimed at predicting sales for the thousands of product families sold at Favorita stores located in Ecuador. The information contained dates, store and product information, whether that item was being promoted, as well as the sales numbers. This is a great use case of Time Series Forecasting.

The model was build using a blending ensemble approach that combines the power of two algorithms and achieved an RMSLE score of 0.404.

Automatic Taxi Dispatcher

Created an AI powered taxi agent to automaically navigate, pick up and drop passengers in a custom grid environment using Open AI Gym and implemted model-free algorithms – Qlearning and SARSA

Newsman

News articles generated by "The Washington Post" was summarized by fine-tuned BERT summarizer or a T5 small summarizer and the model achieved a Rouge score of 0.34. The summarized articles were stored and retrieved using Elasticsearch.

The end-user can retrieve summarized article either using a keyword of interest or a genre of news, ex: politics, sports, technology, etc…

Traffic Assistant

A deep CNN model combined with YOLO v4 is used to detect and identify traffic signs in real time.

The CNN classification model is trained on the GTSRB - German Traffic Sign Recognition Benchmark dataset available on Kaggle. The model was trained on more than 50K images of 43 different classes.

Yolo v4 was trained using Traffic Signs Dataset in YOLO format from Kaggle. Each image in the dataset has its own bounding box coordinates used for YOLO training. Overall, the model achieved 98% accuracy in correctly classifying the traffic signs.
